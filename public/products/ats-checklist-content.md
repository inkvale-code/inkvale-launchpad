## THE 30-POINT ATS OPTIMIZATION CHECKLIST

### SECTION 1: FILE FORMAT & STRUCTURE (Points 1-5)

**‚òê 1. Save your resume as .docx or PDF**
The two most ATS-compatible formats are .docx (Microsoft Word) and PDF. Avoid .pages, .odt, or image-based PDFs. When in doubt, .docx is the safest choice for maximum compatibility across systems.

**‚òê 2. Use standard section headings**
ATS systems look for familiar headers like "Work Experience," "Education," "Skills," and "Summary." Avoid creative titles like "My Journey" or "Where I've Been" ‚Äì these confuse parsing algorithms.

**‚òê 3. Choose a simple, single-column layout**
Multi-column layouts, text boxes, and tables can scramble your information when parsed. Stick to a clean, top-to-bottom, single-column format for optimal ATS readability.

**‚òê 4. Use standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)**
Fancy or decorative fonts may not render correctly in ATS systems. Stick to professional, widely-supported fonts at 10-12pt size for body text.

**‚òê 5. Keep file size under 1MB**
Large files can fail to upload or parse correctly. Compress images if included, and avoid embedded graphics that bloat file size.

### SECTION 2: KEYWORD OPTIMIZATION (Points 6-10)

**‚òê 6. Mirror exact phrases from the job description**
Copy and paste key requirements from the posting into your resume where truthful. If they say "project management," use "project management" ‚Äì not "managed projects."

**‚òê 7. Include industry-specific certifications and acronyms**
List certifications exactly as they appear in standards (PMP, CPA, AWS Certified, etc.). Include both acronym and full name when first mentioned.

**‚òê 8. Add a skills section with 15-20 relevant keywords**
Create a dedicated "Skills" or "Core Competencies" section listing both hard and soft skills mentioned in the job description. This creates keyword density without stuffing.

**‚òê 9. Use action verbs with measurable results**
Start bullet points with strong verbs (led, developed, increased, reduced) followed by specific achievements. Example: "Increased sales by 34% in Q2 2023."

**‚òê 10. Avoid keyword stuffing**
Don't list skills you don't have or repeat the same keyword excessively. ATS algorithms can flag manipulation, and human reviewers will notice immediately.

### SECTION 3: CONTENT FORMATTING (Points 11-15)

**‚òê 11. Write your contact info as plain text**
Never use headers/footers for contact information ‚Äì ATS often can't parse these sections. Place name, phone, email, and LinkedIn URL at the top as standard text.

**‚òê 12. Use standard bullet points (‚Ä¢ or -)**
Avoid decorative symbols, checkmarks, or custom bullets. Stick to standard round bullets or simple dashes that all systems recognize.

**‚òê 13. Format dates consistently (MM/YYYY)**
Use the same date format throughout: "01/2020 - 12/2023" or "January 2020 - December 2023." Inconsistent formatting confuses parsers.

**‚òê 14. Spell out acronyms on first use**
Write "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)" before using "SEO" alone. This ensures ATS catches both the acronym and full term.

**‚òê 15. Avoid images, logos, and graphics**
ATS cannot read images. Your headshot, company logos, or design elements will be ignored and may interfere with text parsing.

### SECTION 4: SECTION ORGANIZATION (Points 16-20)

**‚òê 16. Lead with a strong professional summary**
Open with 3-4 sentences highlighting your years of experience, key skills, and career achievements. This gives ATS and humans immediate context.

**‚òê 17. List work experience in reverse chronological order**
Most recent position first, working backward. Include company name, job title, dates, and 3-5 achievement-focused bullets per role.

**‚òê 18. Include education with degree types spelled out**
Write "Bachelor of Science in Computer Science" not "BS CompSci." Include institution name, location, graduation year, and GPA if above 3.5 and recent.

**‚òê 19. Create a dedicated "Certifications" section if relevant**
If you have professional certifications, give them their own section rather than burying them in education or experience.

**‚òê 20. Place your skills section near the top**
Put your skills/core competencies section high on page one (after summary, before or after experience). This ensures ATS catches keywords early.

### SECTION 5: COMMON ATS MISTAKES TO AVOID (Points 21-25)

**‚òê 21. Don't use headers and footers for content**
ATS typically ignores headers and footers. Keep all important information in the main body of the document.

**‚òê 22. Avoid tables for layout**
While tables might look organized, they scramble content when parsed. Use spacing and formatting instead of table cells.

**‚òê 23. Don't submit your resume as a scanned image PDF**
If your PDF was created by scanning a paper resume, ATS cannot read it. Always create PDFs directly from Word or design software.

**‚òê 24. Skip special characters in file names**
Name your file "FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf" ‚Äì not "R√©sum√©‚òÜ2024‚òÖ.pdf." Special characters can cause upload errors.

**‚òê 25. Don't lie or exaggerate**
While optimizing for ATS is smart, fabricating experience or skills will backfire in interviews. Only include truthful, verifiable information.

### SECTION 6: ADVANCED OPTIMIZATION (Points 26-30)

**‚òê 26. Tailor your resume for each application**
Spend 15-20 minutes customizing your resume for each job, adjusting keywords and emphasis to match the specific role description.

**‚òê 27. Include relevant volunteer work and side projects**
If you lack direct experience, include volunteer roles, freelance projects, or personal initiatives that demonstrate relevant skills.

**‚òê 28. Use LinkedIn URL instead of full address**
Include your LinkedIn profile URL (linkedin.com/in/yourname) rather than a full mailing address to save space and boost online presence.

**‚òê 29. Test your resume through ATS checkers**
Use free tools like Jobscan, Resume Worded, or SkillSyncer to test how well your resume parses before submitting.

**‚òê 30. Keep it to 1-2 pages maximum**
One page for <10 years experience, two pages for senior roles. ATS can handle multiple pages, but recruiters prefer concise, relevant information.

## BONUS: TOP 5 ATS SYSTEMS COMPARISON

### 1. **Taleo (Oracle)**
- Market leader used by large enterprises
- Strong keyword matching capabilities
- Best practices: Use exact keyword phrases, avoid tables

### 2. **Workday**
- Modern interface, AI-enhanced screening
- Parses complex formatting better than older systems
- Best practices: Still use simple layout, strong action verbs

### 3. **Greenhouse**
- Popular with tech companies and startups
- Good at parsing diverse formats
- Best practices: Emphasize skills section, quantify achievements

### 4. **iCIMS**
- Used by mid-to-large companies
- Traditional parsing, prefers standard formats
- Best practices: Conservative formatting, standard section names

### 5. **Lever**
- Tech-forward ATS with strong parsing
- Better at handling creative layouts (but don't risk it)
- Best practices: Focus on keyword density, measurable results
